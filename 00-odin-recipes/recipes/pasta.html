Pesto Pasta

Description

Pesto pasta is easy to make and full of flavor. It tastes good hot or cold!

Ingredients

For the Pesto Sauce:

  • 2 cups fresh basil leaves (packed)
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/3 cup pine nuts (or substitute with walnuts or almonds)
  • 2-3 cloves garlic (peeled)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper (to taste)
  • Juice of half a lemon (optional, for extra brightness)

For the Pasta:

  • 12 ounces pasta (such as spaghetti, fusilli, or penne)
  • Salt (for pasta water)
  • 2 tablespoons pasta cooking water (reserved)
  • Extra Parmesan cheese (for serving)
  • Cherry tomatoes or sautéed vegetables (optional, for garnish)
  • Extra basil leaves (optional, for garnish)

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the pasta and cook according to the package instructions until al dente. Reserve 2 tablespoons of the pasta cooking water, then drain the pasta and set aside.
  2. While the pasta is cooking, prepare the pesto sauce. In a food processor or blender, combine the basil leaves, Parmesan cheese, pine nuts, garlic, and lemon juice (if using). Pulse until finely chopped.
  3. With the processor running, slowly add the olive oil in a steady stream until the mixture becomes smooth and well combined. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste.
  4. In a large mixing bowl, toss the cooked pasta with the pesto sauce. If the sauce is too thick, add a little of the reserved pasta cooking water to thin it out until it reaches your desired consistency.
  5. Serve the pesto pasta warm, topped with extra grated Parmesan cheese, cherry tomatoes, sautéed vegetables, and basil leaves if desired.
  6. Enjoy your delicious homemade Pesto Pasta!
